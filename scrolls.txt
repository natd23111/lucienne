SDG 4 aims to ensure inclusive and equitable quality education and promote lifelong learning opportunities for all by 2030.
Over 244 million children and youth are still out of school worldwide.
Gender equality is a specific target within SDG 4 to ensure girls and boys have equal access to education.
Inclusive education means students with disabilities have equal access to learning in mainstream classrooms.
Digital literacy and the ability to use technology are becoming key parts of quality education globally.
Early Childhood Development programs prepare children for primary education and build lifelong foundations.
Technical and vocational skills for employment help youth and adults secure decent work and livelihoods.
The global teacher gap refers to the shortage of millions of qualified teachers needed to achieve SDG 4.
Literacy and numeracy are the fundamental foundations for all future learning in every country.
Quality education breaks cycles of poverty and reduces inequality within and among nations.
Lack of basic infrastructure like electricity, water, and sanitation in schools is a major barrier in developing nations.
Equity in education means giving each student what they need to reach the same learning outcome, not the same resource.
