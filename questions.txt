Category: SDG 4 Fundamentals (The Basics)
Multiple Choice: What is the primary goal of SDG 4?

A) To build more schools

B) To ensure inclusive and equitable quality education

C) To make all education free for everyone

D) To replace teachers with AI

Answer: B

True/False: SDG 4 aims to promote lifelong learning opportunities for all, not just children.

Answer: True

Multiple Choice: By what year does the UN aim to achieve the SDG 4 targets?

A) 2025

B) 2040

C) 2030

D) 2050

Answer: C

True/False: "Equity" in education means giving every single student the exact same resource, regardless of their individual needs.

Answer: False (Equity means giving what is needed to reach the same outcome).

Multiple Choice: Which of these is considered a "foundation" for all future learning under SDG 4?

A) High-speed internet

B) Literacy and numeracy

C) Expensive textbooks

D) School uniforms

Answer: B

Category: Inclusive & Equitable Learning
Multiple Choice: What does "Inclusive Education" mean in the context of SDG 4?

A) Only teaching the smartest students

B) Ensuring students with disabilities have equal access to learning

C) Separating students based on their background

D) Making education available only in cities

Answer: B

True/False: Gender equality is a specific target within SDG 4 to ensure girls and boys have equal access to education.

Answer: True

Multiple Choice: Which group is often at the highest risk of being "left behind" in education systems?

A) Children in wealthy urban areas

B) Children in vulnerable situations, such as refugees

C) University professors

D) Professional athletes

Answer: B

True/False: SDG 4 only focuses on primary and secondary school, ignoring university or vocational training.

Answer: False

Multiple Choice: Why is "Early Childhood Development" (Pre-school) included in SDG 4?

A) To give parents a break

B) To prepare children for primary education

C) To start testing children earlier

D) It is not actually part of SDG 4

Answer: B

Category: Global Literacy & Skills
Multiple Choice: What is a major barrier to quality education in developing nations?

A) Too many teachers

B) Lack of basic infrastructure like electricity and water in schools

C) Students having too much free time

D) Excessive library books

Answer: B

True/False: Literacy rates globally have been steadily decreasing over the last 50 years.

Answer: False

Multiple Choice: What kind of skills does SDG 4 aim to increase for youth and adults?

A) Only physical labor skills

B) Technical and vocational skills for employment

C) Video gaming skills

D) Skills for memorizing old history dates only

Answer: B

True/False: Digital literacy (the ability to use technology) is becoming a key part of "Quality Education."

Answer: True

Multiple Choice: What is the "Global Teacher Gap"?

A) The physical distance between a teacher and a student

B) The shortage of qualified teachers needed to achieve SDG 4

C) The difference in height between teachers

D) A new type of school uniform

Answer: B
